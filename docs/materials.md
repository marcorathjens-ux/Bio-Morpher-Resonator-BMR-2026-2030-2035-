# Material Specification: BMR Composite Hybrid
## Structural Durability & Bio-Inertness
**Vector:** 2026-2030 (Mechanical Core)

---

## 1. Core Material: CFRP/GFRP Hybrid Laminate
To achieve the necessary balance between **stiffness** (to generate energy) and **flexibility** (to prevent brittle fracture), the BMR utilizes a multi-layer hybrid approach.

### 1.1 Laminate Architecture
*   **Outer Layers:** High-Modulus Carbon Fiber (CFRP) at 0°/45° orientation for torsional stability and maximum energy return.
*   **Inner Core:** S-Glass Fiber (GFRP) for high impact resistance and "forgiveness" during extreme elastic deformation (Storm-Vector).
*   **Matrix:** High-toughness **PEEK (Polyether ether ketone)** or Bio-based Thermoplastics.
    *   *Advantage:* Unlike brittle Epoxy, Thermoplastics can be inductively re-melted for the Phase 2 (2035+) EHD upgrade.

---

## 2. Flexure Joint (The Solid-State Bearing)
The base of the stem is a **variable-thickness profile**, engineered to distribute stress over a parabolic curve.

### 2.1 Fatigue Mitigation
*   **Interlayer Damping:** Integration of 0.2mm **Viscoelastic Elastomer layers** between the CFRP plies. 
*   **Purpose:** These layers act as internal shock absorbers, converting micro-vibrations into negligible heat, thus preventing delamination over 20+ years of operation.

---

## 3. Surface & Environmental Protection (M2 Axiom)
The "skin" of the BMR leaf is designed for harsh outdoor environments (UV, Salt-Spray, Rain-Erosion).

*   **UV-Shield:** Fluorpolymer (ETFE) top-coating (self-cleaning / Lotus-effect).
*   **Conductive Seed:** Sub-surface **Graphene-layer** for lightning protection and preparation for EHD ion emission (Phase 2).
*   **End-of-Life:** 100% Recyclable. The thermoplastic matrix allows for shredding and re-molding, adhering to the **Symbiotic Methodology** of circular resource management.

---

The chosen materials provide a **Safety Factor of 3.5** against static failure and a theoretical **infinite fatigue life** under standard operational amplitudes (Stress < 30% UTS).
